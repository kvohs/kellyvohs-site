Sunday Letter — No. 175 — September 14, 2025

Not Today Monkey

I got a note from a good friend in a group chat this week:

Someone else said, “explain”

The world has lots of reasons why you won’t get what you want.
Don’t let yourself be the reason something doesn’t happen.
Don’t let that monkey in your brain win. Say, “Not today monkey, not today.”

It got me to thinking of a few ways I’ve sabotaged myself. Maybe these resonate.

  • Comparing to others. Use them as examples, not barriers.

  • Quitting when it gets hard.

  • Taking myself too seriously.

  • Worrying about what people think.

  • Believing I’m not qualified.

  • Waiting for permission - from myself or others.

  • Trying to be perfect.

  • Fearing failure or looking foolish.

  • Forgetting that what I want 10 years from now, starts today.

  • Confusing struggle or discomfort with ability or potential.

  • Forgetting that my inner voice usually carries more weight. Whether it’s cheering me on or tearing me down.

Also, I’ve learned to hold these two powers close:

Compounding matters: Getting better each day compounds into excellence. You can go backwards on somedays, but if you just try to get a little better each day, it adds up.

Looking foolish is a superpower: Most people never start because they were afraid of being the student or the novice or the one who says, “I don’t know.”

There isn’t an area of my life where all of this hasn’t shown up for me. At home, at work, and everywhere in between. One area this really stands out for me is with my photography.

The comparison to people who have more reps.
The inability to accept myself.
Trying to make it perfect, instead of just better.
And then finally one day realizing that I’m me, they are them, and everyone started with bad composition, blurry photos, and chasing shots everyone else has.

Just do you.
Find the emotion.
No one can compete with you on being you.

The people you admire started as a beginner.
From novice to expert.
From where they didn’t want to be to where they always hoped to be.
Their key, they didn’t self select out.
They didn’t let the monkey win.
