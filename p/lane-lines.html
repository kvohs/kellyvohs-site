Sunday Letter — No. 177 — September 28, 2025

Lane Lines

Winning mattered to her. She was tough and clear.

But helping us win was what she really cared about.

That tension made her special. You could feel it. You respected her. You listened to her. It didn’t mean you liked what she told you to do, but you did it. Maybe most good coaches are built from that same contradiction.

She was probably in her 60s. When you’re 10, everyone seems older than they really are.

We’d walk up a worn path in the grass from school to the pool. The pool was brilliantly blue against the green hill. Most afternoons were spent in that water. We were young. Eager. Worries came later.

I had just touched the wall and lifted my head to see her motioning us together in the center lanes. I ducked under the worn plastic blue lane line. 

She stood on the edge of the pool between two starting blocks. Calm. Stoic. Coach mode turned all the way up. We all looked up at her. 

“What’s the quickest way from one point to another?”

Twenty-five little heads were frozen. A few hesitant answers floated up. No one was right. We were too young to see the simple answer. We were overthinking it.

“A straight line.”

Looking back, I see her pacing the pool deck during warm-ups, watching swimmers snake their way across those beautiful fifty meters. I know I did.

I remember feeling a little dumb when she gave us the answer. That lesson stuck with me. I think about it all the time. When I sense myself drifting off course, I think about her. I can see the lines in her face and a hint of a smile that says, “it’s happening.” Off course.

Distraction. Doubt. Procrastination.Fear. Whatever.

Another time, she laid down wisdom, her patience touched with frustration.

“Just swim. Don’t look to the left or right. Just swim. Swim well.”

“What’s happening in the other lane doesn’t matter. It’s your lane that matters. Stay in it. Swim straight. Swim hard. That’s it.”

Pick a lane. Swim hard. Breathe. Swim.

Take care. Be good.

Kelly
