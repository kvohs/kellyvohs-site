Sunday Letter — No. 173 — August 31, 2025

Ice Cream & Time Travel

It’s nice to when someone agrees with you. Like ice cream. Sweet, easy, smooth going down. You want more. But too much ice cream is not good. Same with validation. We’re quick to overindulge in our biases.

I was going up my favorite hill. Hay to the right. Puffy summer clouds ahead. Apple trees down to the left. Derek Sivers was agreeing with me. He proceeded to get me back on the daily habit of journaling. He said it’s the one thing everyone can benefit from. That may be true, but if you can’t swallow that absolute, here’s why I think it matters.

Journaling forces you to notice. What you had for dinner. How you feel this morning. What’s happening around you. It doesn’t have to be perfect, but it does require stopping to pay attention.

Journaling helps you remember. Memory is always colored by how you feel now. Words don’t lie. If you want to understand how you felt in a job, a relationship, or during a particular period of your life, your journal won’t lie. And it captures the small things we usually forget:

River swims.

PSL runs.

Tacos with friends three years ago.

That time your kid said you were right.

Maybe you’ll give it a try. Or if you’re like me, you’ve realized your habit fell off and you want to start again. Don’t start with a Tolstoy monologue. Start with five lines before bed. Or dictate them in the car. Keep it small. Keep it doable. For me, locking the habit back in is the priority.

Here’s the real magic for me. Going back. Reading my journal shows me the old me. Sometimes it’s wonderful, like a big family dinner. Sometimes not so great, like an early morning rant about all my frustrations. Either way, it’s useful.

I’m sharing because I know it works for me. Journaling is time travel. Sometimes you revisit joy, sometimes mistakes, sometimes pain. All useful. The 80-year-old me will be grateful. I’m back at it, streak day #7.

Kelly

PS: I bounce between handwriting, tablet, and typing. Mostly I type because it’s faster. It helps me defrag my hard drive. But however I do it, it all goes into Day One.

PPS: A friend once gave me the advice to save compliments where I can go back and read them when I need it. They go in my journal too.
