## What I've Learned About Building Teams

At LivCor we ran hundreds of distributed communities, each with its own culture, team, and identity. My job was never to have all the answers. It was to build the right team, get the right people in the room, and then move together with speed and conviction. That's how I try to lead. Rarely perfectly, but always trying to get better. Build the team. Clarity, alignment, resources, emotional connectedness. Best idea wins. Teams win.

I think we proved that you can do well and do good at the same time.

## How I Lead

I believe in an order of operations: people, product, performance. Get a great team, you'll have a great product. Have a great product, you'll get great performance. That sequence matters.

I learned early -- at The Breakers, then in the army -- that it's *we*, not *me*. Build the team. Give them expectations, process, voice, and care. You'll find that team has superpowers. I still believe that.

At the Boca Raton Resort, 39 days in, I was sitting on my office floor staring at 67 projects on a whiteboard. There was no way we could do all of it. Then I said out loud: *do what you can every day.* If we get 1% better each day, it compounds. That became Better Today Than Yesterday. That's how I try to run everything now. I write about it weekly [here](https://kellyvohs.substack.com).

I believe in leadership by working around, not management by walking around. You have to actually do the thing.
