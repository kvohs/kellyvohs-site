Privacy

type is a sheet of paper. Paper doesn't phone home.

Your writing

Everything you type stays on your device. Kept pages are saved as plain markdown files — on the Mac, to a folder you choose; on iPhone, to your own iCloud Drive (or to the phone itself if iCloud is off). They sync through your Apple account, on Apple's infrastructure, the same as any file you put in iCloud Drive. There is no type server. Your pages never pass through one, and no one — including me — can read them.

No accounts, no tracking

There is nothing to sign up for and nothing watching you write. No analytics, no telemetry, no crash harvesting, no advertising identifiers, no cookies. The app doesn't know who you are and doesn't try to find out.

The one thing that leaves your device

If you choose to send feedback from inside the app, the text you wrote in the feedback box — and the app's version number — is sent to me, after showing you exactly what's about to be sent. That's the whole payload. Include your email if you'd like a reply; otherwise it's anonymous.

TestFlight

If you're running a beta through TestFlight, Apple may share crash reports and the feedback you submit through TestFlight itself with me, under Apple's own privacy terms. That's Apple's pipeline, not something built into type.

Questions? Write to kelly@kellyvohs.com. This policy covers the type apps for macOS and iOS. Last updated 12 June 2026.
